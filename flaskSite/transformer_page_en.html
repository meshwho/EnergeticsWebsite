Underground Transformers

Main Goals

The primary targets of Russian attacks on infrastructure are power transformers and generation facilities. During the war, more than half of Ukraine's power transformers were destroyed, resulting in power outages for millions of people.

Transformers are key elements of the energy system. The destruction of a high-voltage substation, such as 330 kV, can leave an entire city without power.

Placing a transformer underground allows it to be protected from external factors, eliminating issues such as noise, electromagnetic waves, the need for land acquisition for substation construction, and more.

The main issue with such placement is the heat generated by the transformer. Due to the lack of air circulation, heat accumulates, leading to transformer overheating and failure.
